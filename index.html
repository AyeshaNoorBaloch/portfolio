Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard

Business Problem

The organization was facing increasing employee turnover and needed to identify the departments, age groups, and employee segments most likely to leave.

Analysis Performed

  • Department-wise attrition analysis
  • Age-group attrition trends
  • Gender-based attrition comparison
  • Job satisfaction impact analysis
  • Education field attrition analysis

Key Insights

  • R&D and Sales departments experienced the highest turnover.
  • Employees aged 25–34 showed the highest attrition levels.
  • Lower job satisfaction strongly correlated with employee exits.
  • Life Sciences and Medical graduates had higher attrition rates.

Business Outcome

Recommended targeted retention programs and employee engagement initiatives, helping HR identify high-risk employee segments and reduce future turnover costs.

Tools: Power BI, Excel, DAX

HR Analytics Dashboard

Business Problem

HR leaders lacked visibility into workforce trends, salary patterns, employee tenure, and attrition drivers.

Analysis Performed

  • Attrition tracking
  • Salary slab analysis
  • Years-at-company trends
  • Education-level analysis
  • Job role performance review

Key Insights

  • Employees earning under 5K showed the highest turnover.
  • Laboratory Technicians and Research Scientists were most affected.
  • Most attrition occurred during the first years of employment.
  • Average employee age remained stable at 37 years.

Business Outcome

Supported compensation review decisions and onboarding improvements aimed at improving employee retention.

Tools: Power BI, Excel, DAX

Compensation Analytics Dashboard

Business Problem

Management required visibility into salary allocation, compensation fairness, bonus distribution, and workforce costs.

Analysis Performed

  • Salary budget utilization
  • Business-unit compensation review
  • Position-wise salary analysis
  • Grade-level salary comparison
  • Gender compensation evaluation

Key Insights

  • Operational departments consumed the largest payroll budgets.
  • Compensation differed significantly across grades.
  • Professional-level roles represented the largest salary expenditure.
  • Salary distribution reflected organizational hierarchy.

Business Outcome

Enabled leadership to evaluate workforce spending and support compensation planning using data-driven insights.

Tools: Power BI, Excel

Marketing & Engagement Analytics Dashboard

Business Problem

Marketing teams needed visibility into engagement performance, traffic sources, content effectiveness, and conversion behavior.

Analysis Performed

  • Social platform performance tracking
  • Traffic source analysis
  • Content-type effectiveness evaluation
  • Conversion funnel monitoring

Key Insights

  • Instagram generated the highest engagement.
  • Stories outperformed other content formats.
  • Traffic volumes dropped significantly through the funnel.
  • LinkedIn delivered strong professional audience engagement.

Business Outcome

Improved campaign optimization strategies and content planning by highlighting the highest-performing channels and formats.

Tools: Power BI, Excel

Executive Workforce Analytics Dashboard

Business Problem

Senior leadership required a consolidated workforce monitoring solution for employee performance, demographics, and retention.

Analysis Performed

  • Departmental workforce review
  • Age-distribution analysis
  • Attrition by role
  • Workforce composition monitoring
  • Performance trend evaluation

Key Insights

  • Most employees belonged to the 26–35 age bracket.
  • Research and technical roles showed higher turnover.
  • Certain departments demonstrated stronger retention.
  • Demographic trends highlighted succession-planning opportunities.

Business Outcome

Supported workforce planning, recruitment prioritization, and long-term talent management strategies.

Tools: Power BI, Excel, DAX
